üìòWhat Are Rocks?

Rocks are hard substances found inside and on the surface of the Earth. They are made up of different minerals that give them various textures, colors, and densities.

üß™Formation of Rocks

Rocks form over millions of years through **sedimentation, pressure, and heat**.
  • Sedimentation: Water carries soil and dead organisms and deposits them as layers.
  • Pressure: Over time, layers press and harden into rock.
  • Heat: Extreme heat inside Earth can change rocks into new forms.

üî•1. Igneous Rocks

Formed when hot molten magma cools and hardens. These are the oldest rocks.

üíß2. Sedimentary Rocks

Formed when wind and water deposit small sediments that get pressed into rock. These rocks often contain **fossils**.

Rock Formation Characteristics Uses
Limestone From shells and bones of dead sea animals. White, red, brown, or black. Cement and lime production.

Shale From silt and clay. Soft, black, fine particles. ‚Äî

6. Answer the following questions:

a. What is the earth composed of?

The Earth is composed of hard substances called rocks and soil.

b. What is rock?

Rocks are the hard substances found inside and on the surface of the Earth. They are made up of minerals that give them different colours, textures, and densities.

c. For what purposes are sedimentary rocks used in your locality?

Sandstone: Used for construction, decoration, and millstones.
Conglomerate: Used for construction of houses.

d. Describe the formation of rocks in short.

Igneous rocks form when magma cools and hardens.
Sedimentary rocks form when sediments carried by water and air settle and get compressed.
Metamorphic rocks form when existing rocks change under high heat and pressure.

e. What type of rocks are granite, pumice and obsidian? For what purposes are they used?

Granite, pumice, and obsidian are all igneous rocks.

Granite: Used for paving roads, stairs, and buildings.
Pumice: Light and porous; formed during volcanic eruptions.
Obsidian: Glassy volcanic rock.
