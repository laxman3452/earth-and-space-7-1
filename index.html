üìòWhat Are Rocks?

Rocks are hard substances found inside and on the surface of the Earth. They are made up of different minerals that give them various textures, colors, and densities.

üß™Formation of Rocks

Rocks form over millions of years through **sedimentation, pressure, and heat**.
  • Sedimentation: Water carries soil and dead organisms and deposits them as layers.
  • Pressure: Over time, layers press and harden into rock.
  • Heat: Extreme heat inside Earth can change rocks into new forms.

üî•1. Igneous Rocks

Formed when hot molten magma cools and hardens. These are the oldest rocks.

üíß2. Sedimentary Rocks

Formed when wind and water deposit small sediments that get pressed into rock. These rocks often contain **fossils**.

3. Give reason.

  • a. **Iodine is mixed in common salt.**
    Reason: Iodine is a vital element for the proper functioning of the **thyroid gland**. Mixing it with common salt ensures adequate dietary intake to prevent deficiency diseases like goitre (swelling of the thyroid gland).
  • b. **Gold is found in a free state in nature.**
    Reason: Gold is a highly **unreactive** (noble) metal. It does not easily combine chemically with air, water, or other substances, allowing it to remain in its pure, free state.
  • c. **Aluminum is used to make body parts of vehicles and aeroplanes.**
    Reason: Aluminum is strong, **resistant to corrosion**, and extremely **lightweight**, which is crucial for reducing fuel consumption and increasing the efficiency of vehicles and aeroplanes.
  • d. **The handle of a pressure cooker is covered with hard plastic.**
    Reason: Hard plastic is a **bad conductor (insulator)** of heat. This prevents heat transfer from the hot metal body to the user's hand, allowing the handle to be held safely.
  • e. **A flame of fire is seen at night time in the area where Sulphur is present.**
    Reason: Hills having **sulphur** come in contact with air, causing the sulphur to burn.
